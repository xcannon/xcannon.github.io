Wolfgang A. Mozart

Singles

Here are some of Wolfgang’s works that I found as separate pieces, though some are actual stand-alones & others are not.  The first one is, the third isn’t (it’s a truncated version of part 2 of Piano Concerto No. 20).  The second one, Andante in F, is subheaded as being “composed for the mechanical organ” ... did they have pizza parlors back then?  Anyway, the organ version is presented here.  Rondo in D is the only classical piece on this site that I overtly imposed my own will on, in this case by ignoring the front & back repeats.  (It’s good, but not 9 minutes worth of good.)  The Suite in C is unfinished: Mozart was inspired by a large collection of Bach works appearing in Vienna, but evidently lost interest after completing 3 parts (a proper suite would have had—probably—4 more parts).  Sophie & Konstanze, like Allegretto in A on another page, was incomplete at his death & given a wrap-up by someone else, I know not whom.  The title is due to WM writing the names of his daughter & wife in the margin above 6 slow, plaintive notes in the short minor-key section ... as if he wanted so sing their names there.
