Sonatina Saga



Muzio Clementi’s 6 sonatinas Op. 36 of 1798 were extremely popular in their time, & are still a favorite in the student repertoire.  On the old Interweb I stumbled across the sheet music for what were reputed to be follow-ups—2 sets of 3 sonatinas billed as Op. 37 & 38—and had finished sequencing them when my suspicions arose again & I did some following up of my own; I already knew of a set of 3 “big” sonatas called Op. 37.  Turns out someone, back in the 18th century—probably the original publisher—had repackaged & re-released Clementi’s much earlier work (Op. 4) as new material, to capitalize on the success of Op. 36.  This deception was never fully exposed & has apparently survived right down to the present day.

All of which goes to show that the omnipresence of weasels in the music business is nothing new.

They’re very nice pieces, though, so here they are; & the tale ends with a wrap-up of the follow-up: the inclusion of the actual Op. 37, comprising 3 excellent sonatas.
