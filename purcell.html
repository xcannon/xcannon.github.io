Henry Purcell

1659 – 1695

“A Choice Collection” – Harpsichord Suites

Text of title page (mild bemusement).

Englishman Henry Purcell was the author of the respected opera Dido and Aeneas, most famed in his own time for his incidental music for stage plays.  These “lessons” are like a precursor to Bach’s Well-tempered clavier.  Along with Kuhnau & Buxtehude, this is 17th century music, the eldest on the site.
