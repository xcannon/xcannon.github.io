More variations on Müller – The theme & 1st 3 variations comprise Allegretto con variazioni by one August Eberhard Müller, a canon of beginner piano.  The repeats of his 2nd motives are my addition, not in the original; thus his contribution amounts to around 3:20 of this version.  My original variations begin with the 4th one in the minor, & continue through the end.

(It may be noticed, here & elsewhere, that I don’t include the customary pauses between variations.  In fact I dislike them, & much prefer a continuous, unbroken rhythm.)

But why write variations on someone else’s elementary work at all?  Did it seem unfinished, somehow?  Yes, to the 2nd question: the way it ends is a bit clowny & seemed to cry out for follow-up.  On good days, once started I don’t stop until it’s over; that day was a good one.
